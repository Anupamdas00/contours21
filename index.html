About The Department

The Department of Visual Arts was formed in the year 2008. The two-year post-graduate (M.F.A) degree course in Painting conducted by the Department under the Faculty of Music & Fine Arts at the University of Kalyani concentrates on both the traditional and new aspects of practical exploration in this field. The course creates the scope for different dimensions of technical expertise and motivating the students for critical enquiry of the works done in the class-rooms. Concern about the environment, social and cultural issues as well as knowledge of history is stressed upon. Besides the objective of academic oriented tasks, stress is also laid upon future professional advancement of the students. A dedicated team of Faculty comprising of both permanent and guest teachers who have also made a mark in the professional field impart guidance to the students.

Message From The Department

Within the domain of the various practices of Visual Arts a meaningful form is arrived at by a practitioner through the representation of a shape conceived through a concept and moulded by artistic technique. Such construction requires definition through a design that marks the outline of the form. It can create variables and join connecting points to define a ‘contour’ of the form encompassing the concept. Experimentations based upon this concept have given much joy and inter-mingling of associations for the young student-artists who pursue their objectives under the post-graduate course of the Department of Visual Arts, University of Kalyani. Embedded from within a rural-urban setting of the University campus, the art works of the students convey shared cultural references. The past few months due to the extended pandemic situation that we have been facing has somewhat stymied the art activities. But our students have shown commendable patience and mental strength to emerge from the enclosures of the confined situation to pursue and create their ideas upon their selected ideas. They have tried their best to overcome the emotional strain and displacement through their own unique ways to delineate their subjectivity of form. The examples which form a part of ‘Contours 2021’ are an acknowledgement of their untiring efforts and the dedication of the teachers of the Department who have always stood beside them to encourage their attempts. Though hard to explain, but sincere desire and unbridled enthusiasm can create multifaceted models of visual ideas, which may be found in abundance through the creations of our students. We take this opportunity to bless them. We also extend our revered thanks to the University authority and entire fraternity for keeping us in their mind and heart.
